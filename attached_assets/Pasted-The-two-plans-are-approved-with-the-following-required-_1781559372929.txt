2. Verify the exact available Musixmatch endpoints and response formats from the official Musicathon documentation before coding. Do not invent endpoint behavior for synced lyrics, translations, structure, snippets, or mood analysis.

3. Do not permanently store complete lyric or translation arrays inside `MusicMinute`, seed data, local storage, or the database. Persist only the Musixmatch track ID, section name, timing boundaries, line indexes, language reference, and timing offset. Fetch lyric content at runtime.

5. Do not derive “authentic” lyric timings from BPM. Use Musixmatch synced timing when available and manually timed original mock content for fallback demonstrations.

6. Add `timingOffsetMs` to the selected section and include a preview control allowing the creator to shift lyrics earlier or later.

8. Inspect the existing video implementation before changing libraries. If starting a new implementation, prefer `expo-video`. Avoid introducing new dependency on deprecated `expo-av`.
